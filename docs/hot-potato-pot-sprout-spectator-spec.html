1. Reframing the Game

In the current design, the potato is a liability you try to offload. The souvenir is consolation for being stuck. Holding is anxiety; getting bought out is relief.

Add a Pot and the sign flips. The potato becomes an income-bearing asset that pays its holder proportionally to how long they've held. Now holding is yield, and getting bought out is both reward (souvenir + pot payout) and regret (you just lost the income stream).

Add Sprouting on top and the game gains a biological rhythm. The potato ripens. The visual changes. A stagnant hold becomes a public event, not a dead listing. Everyone watching knows the sprouted potato is coming to market at a known discount, and the race restarts.

Add Spectators and the 99% of the audience that was idle now has on-chain skin in the game — without needing to buy the potato itself.

The design shift in one line: from "don't be the last one holding" to "be the one who grew it longest."

2. The Pot — Patience Payouts

2.1 Where the Pot Comes From

Three potential funding sources, not mutually exclusive:

SourceRateProCon
Creator fee split2% of sale (of the current 5%)No increase in player-facing feesHalves your creator revenue
Overpay routing50% of any amount above askingOverpayers fund the prize they're gunning for; narratively tightOnly active when overpay happens
Burn kickbackIf a player burns a souvenir < Epic for a trade-in, 0.01 ETH from their next buy routes to PotTies the burn economy to the PotAdds complexity; small flow

Recommended mix: 1% of every sale (from creator fee) + 50% of overpay amount. Predictable baseline income plus a variable spike from overpayers. Creator fee becomes 4% to holder-adjacent / 1% to pot — phrasing matters: "5% protocol fee, 4% to creator, 1% to the Potato Pot." Players perceive it as a rake that comes back to them, not a tax.

2.2 How the Pot Pays Out

Outgoing holders claim a share of the Pot when they're bought out, tied to their hold tier. Quick flips get nothing from the Pot; long holders extract serious size.

Hold TierDurationPot ShareNotes
Tier 0 Common< 6h0%Quick flips feed the Pot but don't drain it
Tier 1 Rare6 – 48h1%Small reward for overnight commitment
Tier 2 Epic2 – 7 days3%Meaningful payout
Tier 3 Legendary7 – 30 days7%Strong incentive
Tier 4 Max30+ days15%Pot drained hard — rewards ultra-patience

Why these numbers work: with an assumed realistic sale mix (~80% Tier 0, ~15% Tier 1, ~4% Tier 2, ~1% Tier 3+), the Pot drains about 34% of its balance per week while receiving inflow from every sale. At steady-state with the recommended funding mix, the Pot parks at a size that makes a Tier 3 payout meaningfully profitable on its own — see the simulation in §7.

2.3 Why This Creates Competition, Not Just Avoidance

Right now the game asks: "who blinks first?" With the Pot it also asks: "who can outlast the buyout pressure the longest?" The Pot makes patience visibly profitable — players can literally see the Pot grow, see the 15% payout available, and know the exact moment another buyer snipes the position.

This is also the mechanic that makes being bought out feel like being sniped, not rescued. You were earning yield; someone stole your position. That's a clean, universally understood game emotion.

3. The Sprouting Event

Sprouting is what happens when nobody buys. It's the Pot's sibling mechanic — the Pot rewards being held, Sprouting resolves being ignored. Together they cover both directions of activity risk.

3.1 Five Stages of the Potato Life-Cycle

Stages are read from on-chain block.timestamp - lastSaleTime. No backend involvement.

STAGE 1 · DAY 0–3 · FRESH

Normal potato

Default state. Asking price is whatever the current holder set. Full anxiety, full yield accrual.

STAGE 2 · DAY 3–7 · LEAFING

A small leaf emerges on the potato image

Cosmetic signal only. No mechanical change. Tells watchers "this one's been sitting a while." Great for X/Discord — posts asking "anyone gonna buy this leafy boy?"

STAGE 3 · DAY 7–14 · SPROUTED

Price begins decaying — 5%/day toward a floor of 1.1× purchase price

Mechanical state change. The effective asking price is min(explicitAsk, sprouted_price) where sprouted_price is computed on-chain from the current ask and days since sprout began. Anyone who buys during Sprouted gets a +1 Sprout boost on their next souvenir. The potato image shows full sprouts.

STAGE 4 · DAY 14–21 · BLOOMED

Decay accelerates to 10%/day; Sprout boost increases to +2

The discount is now dramatic. The souvenir boost is dramatic. This is the "flash sale moment" — everyone watching knows a bargain is on the table. The Pot grows because the holder is still getting hit with Tier 4 payout potential. Image shows a flowering potato.

STAGE 5 · DAY 21+ · HARVEST WINDOW

The holder can call harvest() for a guaranteed Legendary Grower souvenir

At day 21 the holder gains the option to voluntarily harvest: mint a one-of-a-kind "Grower" souvenir (guaranteed Legendary, visually distinct flowering potato) and reset the listing to 25% of last price for the next buyer. The holder keeps their full Pot payout at Tier 4 rate. This is the dignified exit option — you didn't get bought out, you chose to grow and release.

Day 30 fallback — Treasury Backstop. If no sale AND no harvest by day 30, anyone in the world can call treasuryBuyback(). The treasury (1% of all sales) purchases the potato at 40% of last price, the holder receives the Tier 4 Pot payout plus a Grower souvenir, and the potato is re-listed at a starting price. The caller of the keeper function receives 0.005 ETH as gas bounty. Game never freezes.

3.2 Why Sprouting Works Thematically

The potato is literally a living object that grows if left alone. This is not a mechanical metaphor — it's what potatoes do. That earned narrative buys you a tremendous amount of player tolerance for complexity. "It's sprouted" is a complete explanation; no one needs to ask why.

It also gives your AI image pipeline a reason to generate new art for the same token — a stage 3 sprouted potato, a stage 4 bloomed potato, a stage 5 ready-to-harvest potato. The potato's visual state is dynamic, which is rare and distinctive for an ERC-721.

3.3 Interaction With The Pot

The combination is deliberate: the Pot gives holders reason to hold; Sprouting ensures that holding doesn't break the game. A holder who survives to Stage 4 has:

  • Spent 14+ days earning yield toward a Tier 4 payout (15% of the Pot).
  • Painted a giant target on themselves — everyone can see the discounted sprouted potato.
  • Built up the buyer's counter-incentive (the Sprout +2 boost).
  • Still has a safety valve (Harvest at day 21, Treasury at day 30).

This is the exact shape of a great late-game moment: high stakes, visible clock, multiple actors with conflicting interests, no dead hands.

4. Spectator Mechanics

All proposed spectator actions are on-chain. No backend state. Low gas where possible.

4.1 The Rooting Pool On-chain — the headline spectator mechanic

Any wallet can stake a small amount of ETH behind the current holder. Stakes accumulate while the holder holds. When the holder is bought out, stake outcomes depend on the hold tier achieved — rooters win together or lose together.

Holder ReachedRooter Outcome
Tier 0 (quick flip)Lose stake → flows to the Pot
Tier 1 (6–48h)Stake refunded, no bonus
Tier 2 (2–7 days)Stake + 1.5× from Pot
Tier 3 (7–30 days)Stake + 2× from Pot and +1 boost pending next buy
Tier 4 (30+ days)Stake + 3× from Pot and +2 boost pending next buy

Why this is the best spectator mechanic: it aligns spectators with the holder (so the holder feels publicly cheered for, not just hunted), creates visible social proof ("12 people rooting with 0.3 ETH behind them"), generates organic drama ("will the holder make it to Tier 3?"), and adds a soft-money flow to the Pot when rooters lose. Rooters have both carrot and stick — they can't just root and forget, they have to judge the holder's chances.

Minimum stake: 0.001 ETH. Maximum per wallet per holder: 0.05 ETH (prevents whale-dominance in the rooting pool). Multiple rooters per holder accumulate. Rooters use pull-not-push withdrawal (they call claimRoot() after the holder is bought out) to keep gas bounded.

4.2 The Glance On-chain — free engagement floor

Any wallet can call glance() once per day for the cost of gas only. The contract increments a per-wallet counter. At milestones (10, 50, 200, 1000 glances) the wallet is awarded a +1 boost claim on their next buy. This is pure habit formation — show up, tap the button, build toward a future advantage. Low stakes, high frequency, creates a Discord/X ritual of "did you glance today?"

4.3 The Limit Buy On-chain

Spectators can place on-chain conditional orders: "auto-buy the potato if it drops below X ETH" or "auto-buy if the potato enters Stage 3 Sprouted." Funds are held in escrow. First matching order executes. This creates:

  • Market depth — the potato is never truly unbought because limit buys are waiting.
  • Sprout-triggered flash trades — when a Stage 3 transition happens, all stage-conditional limits fire and someone wins the snipe.
  • An additional reason for late-game visitors to set-and-forget — lower the barrier to participation.

4.4 Souvenir Vote On-chain

Weekly, any wallet can cast one vote for its favorite souvenir in the gallery. Winning souvenir's current owner earns +1 boost + a small cut of the Pot (0.5%). Creates collection curation content, drives OpenSea traffic, and makes rare souvenirs socially valuable beyond their rarity tier.

4.5 Rooting-Only Referrals On-chain

Fix the referral farming problem from the prior review by requiring the referrer to have rooted at least once in the current chapter. Referrals then become a signal that the referrer has real skin in the game. Kills the influencer-spam vector at its root.

5. On-Chain Architecture

Here's what moves on-chain, what gets added to contract state, and what events fire. This is a minimum-viable implementation; your real Solidity will be more defensive.

5.1 New Contract State

uint256 public pot;                              // cumulative Pot balance
uint256 public treasury;                         // Day-30 backstop fund
uint256 public lastSaleTime;                     // already exists
uint256 public lastSalePrice;                    // for sprout decay math

mapping(address => uint256) public pendingBoosts;       // merged boost slot
mapping(address => uint256) public glanceCount;         // per-wallet
mapping(address => uint256) public lastGlanceTime;      // rate-limit to 1/day

// Rooting pool
mapping(address => mapping(address => uint256)) public rootedAmount;  // rooter => holder => stake
mapping(address => address[]) public rootersOf;                      // holder => list of rooters
mapping(address => uint256) public claimableRoot;                    // pull-withdraw

// Limit buys
struct LimitOrder {
    address buyer;
    uint256 maxPrice;
    uint8 triggerStage;      // 0 = price-only, 3 = sprout, 4 = bloom
    uint256 escrow;
}
LimitOrder[] public limitOrders;

5.2 Key Function Signatures

// Buy flow (modified) — distributes to pot, rooters, creator, previous holder
function buyPotato(uint256 newAskingPrice) external payable;

// Sprouting read — pure view of current state
function sproutStage() public view returns (uint8);
function currentAskingPrice() public view returns (uint256);  // handles decay

// Harvest — holder voluntarily ends Stage 4+
function harvest() external;   // only callable by current holder after day 21

// Treasury backstop — keeper function, bounty to caller
function treasuryBuyback() external;  // only callable after day 30

// Pot patience payout — called inside buyPotato()
function _payPotPatience(address holder, uint8 tier) internal;

// Rooting
function root(address holder) external payable;
function unroot(address holder) external;   // refund if current holder still holding
function claimRoot() external;              // pull reward after buyout

// Glance
function glance() external;

// Limit buy
function placeLimit(uint256 maxPrice, uint8 triggerStage) external payable;
function executeLimits() external;   // anyone can trigger; pays gas bounty

5.3 Events to Fire (critical for frontend + spectators)

event PotatoSold(address from, address to, uint256 price, uint8 holdTier, uint256 potPayout);
event SproutStageChanged(uint8 newStage);
event Harvested(address grower, uint256 growerSouvenirId);
event TreasuryBuyback(address caller, uint256 price, uint256 bounty);
event Rooted(address rooter, address holder, uint256 amount);
event RootClaimed(address rooter, uint256 reward);
event Glanced(address watcher, uint256 newTotal);
event LimitPlaced(uint256 orderId, address buyer, uint256 maxPrice, uint8 stage);
event LimitExecuted(uint256 orderId);

5.4 Gas Considerations

Two hot spots:

  • Rooter distribution on sale. Iterating rootersOf[holder] inside buyPotato would make the buy unpredictably expensive. Use pull-withdraw: on sale, write each rooter's outcome to claimableRoot[rooter] and emit one event. Rooters then call claimRoot() themselves and pay their own gas. Write loop is still O(rooters), but rooter count is capped by the per-wallet max stake and pool will be small enough (dozens, not thousands).
  • Limit order matching. Don't match inside buyPotato — make it a separate keeper. Anyone calling executeLimits() gets a small bounty. This externalizes gas cost to actors who actually benefit.

7. Economic Simulation

To make sure the numbers aren't aspirational, here's a simple steady-state model. Assumptions:

  • Average of 100 sales per week.
  • Average sale price: 0.1 ETH.
  • Average overpay rate: 25% above asking.
  • Tier distribution of sales: 80% Tier 0, 15% Tier 1, 4% Tier 2, 1% Tier 3+.

7.1 Pot Inflow

  • 1% of every sale = 100 × 0.001 ETH = 0.1 ETH/week
  • 50% of overpay = 100 × 0.0125 ETH = 1.25 ETH/week
  • Total inflow: ~1.35 ETH/week

7.2 Pot Outflow

TierSales/weekDrain rateWeekly drain
Tier 0800%0
Tier 1151%0.15 × pot
Tier 243%0.12 × pot
Tier 317%0.07 × pot
Total100~0.34 × pot

7.3 Steady-State Pot Size

Inflow = Outflow → 1.35 = 0.34 × pot_size → pot_size ≈ 4.0 ETH

7.4 What That Pays Out

A player who...Wins from PotIn USD (ETH=$3000)
Holds overnight (Tier 1)0.04 ETH~$120
Holds 3 days (Tier 2)0.12 ETH~$360
Holds 10 days (Tier 3)0.28 ETH~$840
Holds 30+ days (Tier 4)0.60 ETH~$1,800

Interpretation: these Pot payouts are in addition to the 95% sale proceeds and the souvenir. A 10-day holder is getting bought out at a ~40% premium (15% compounding for 10 days), getting an Epic/Legendary souvenir, and pulling ~$840 from the Pot. That's a very attractive hold. At the same time a quick flipper earns nothing from the Pot and provides its inflow. The Pot's purpose — redistributing value from impatient flippers to patient holders — is structurally aligned with the game's aesthetic goal.

7.5 Scaling

Pot size scales linearly with both sale volume and price. At 10× the assumed activity, the Pot steady-state is ~40 ETH and a Tier 4 payout is 6 ETH. The mechanic gets more compelling as the game grows — no retuning required.

8. Edge Cases & Risks

R1 — Buyer-Holder Collusion

A coordinated pair: Alice holds for 30+ days, Bob then buys her out. Alice pulls a Tier 4 Pot payout. Bob then quickly re-sells back to Alice in a short hold. Alice holds 30+ days again. Repeat. They drain the Pot to each other.

Mitigation: the Pot payout is already capped by Pot size. The first drain to 15% doesn't empty the Pot; subsequent drains get smaller. More importantly, Alice and Bob each pay the creator fee + treasury fee + overpay fees on every round trip. The collusion has real cost. Also: cap Pot payouts to the same wallet at 2× in any 90-day window — after two Tier 4 hits, further Tier 4 payouts downgrade to Tier 2 rates. Clear on-chain rule, kills the farm.

R2 — Rooting Pool Manipulation

A holder self-roots (via alt wallet) to amplify their own payout. Mitigation: disallow the current holder (or any wallet that bought the potato in the last 30 days) from rooting for themselves or current holder. Enforced on-chain by address check in root().

R3 — Limit-Buy Front-Running

A pending limit buy at 0.5 ETH sitting in the contract is visible on-chain. A frontrunner could buy the potato at 0.49 and immediately sell to the limit at 0.5. Mitigation: limit orders only auto-execute when triggered conditions (price or stage) are met. The frontrunner path is equivalent to a normal buy-and-flip; the limit holder still pays what they bid, the potato still trades. This isn't actually exploitable, just market mechanics. Worth flagging in player docs.

R4 — Sprout-Snipe Arms Race

Stage 3 transitions suddenly make the potato attractive. Bots will race to the block where the stage changes. That's fine — it's the point. But regular humans won't win those races. Mitigation: add a 1-block "dutch window" at stage entry where buys revert — gives human UIs a chance to warn their users. Or: make the first post-stage buy use a 1-block commit/reveal to level the playing field. Optional; depends on how protective you want to be of retail.

R5 — Treasury Drain

If many successive sales happen right after treasury buybacks, the treasury can deplete. Mitigation: treasury buyback is a rare event (requires 30 days of zero activity). In practice if the game has activity at all, treasury refills faster than it drains. If the game truly dies, the last treasury buyback is a graceful wind-down, not a bug.

R6 — Harvest Exploit

Players might collude to stall past day 21 to harvest a guaranteed Legendary, then re-buy cheaply. Mitigation: a wallet that has harvested cannot harvest again for the next 180 days (enforced by lastHarvestTime[msg.sender] check). Additionally, Grower souvenirs are a separate metadata track — rare and named as "Grower #N" — so repeated harvests by one wallet are publicly visible and socially unflattering.

Why this stack resolves the earlier critique: the Pot solves H1 (hold has real economic meaning again) and partially solves C4 (late-game holder gets a real reward, not just consolation). Sprouting solves C3 (no grief-lock), gives you the "sprouting event" you already wanted, and opens up the Stage 4 Bloom as a re-engagement event. The Rooting Pool solves H2 (spectators have something to do) and H3 partially (the buyout event becomes a payout event for a whole crowd). All of it is on-chain, all of it is enforceable, and none of it requires the backend to hold game-critical state.
