🥔 HOW TO PLAY

The game in one page

"It's not about holding out. It's about how long you can stay on the throne."

The Whole Game In 30 Seconds

The Hot Potato is a throne. One player sits on it at a time. While you sit, a Pot of ETH beneath the throne accumulates your claim. Anyone can pay to snatch the throne from you at any moment. When they do, you leave with:

  • The ETH they paid you for your seat (minus a 5% protocol fee)
  • A Souvenir NFT whose rarity scales with how long you sat
  • A slice of the Pot based on your sit-tier — more time = bigger slice
You never defend the potato.
You just try to stay on it as long as possible.

Who Gets What, When

💸
THE BUYER
Just took the throne

Paid ETH → now holds the potato. Doesn't touch the current Pot.

What they do get right now:

  • The throne itself
  • A fresh sit-timer at 0:00
  • A Sprout Boost (+1 or +2) if they bought during a sprouted potato

Their Pot slice and souvenir come later, when they get sniped.

👑
THE HOLDER
Currently on the throne

Sit-timer ticking up. Pot slice growing. Getting sniped is inevitable — the only question is when.

What they pay out on snipe:

  • 95% of the next buyer's ETH
  • A Souvenir NFT (rarity from sit-tier + boosts)
  • A slice of the Pot (0% → 15% based on tier)
🍿
THE SPECTATOR
Watching the throne

Can't afford to buy? Don't want to? Still in the game.

  • Root ETH on the current holder — win big if they last
  • Glance daily — build boosts just by showing up
  • Place a Limit Buy — auto-buy when the price drops or the potato sprouts

MENTAL MODEL

Every second you're on the throne, a tiny fraction of the Pot is quietly becoming yours. You can see it fill on the screen. You'll never know when you get sniped — but you'll know exactly what you leave with the instant it happens.

Where Does The Pot Come From?

Two inflows, both taken automatically on every sale:

  • 1% of every sale price — paid out of the standard 5% protocol fee. You're not paying extra; a fifth of the fee gets redirected back to players.
  • 50% of any overpay — if you pay more than the asking price (to grab a boost), half of that extra goes straight into the Pot.

So: the Pot is funded by impatient flippers and big overpayers. It's paid out to patient holders. That's the whole economy in one sentence.

The Sprouting Event

If nobody snipes the throne for a while, the potato starts to grow. Five stages, all visible on the site:

  • Day 0–3 · Fresh. Normal potato.
  • Day 3–7 · Leafing. A small leaf appears. Cosmetic only — signals "this one's been sitting."
  • Day 7–14 · Sprouted. Asking price decays 5%/day. Any buyer gets a +1 Sprout Boost on their next souvenir. The race is on.
  • Day 14–21 · Bloomed. Decay accelerates to 10%/day. Sprout Boost jumps to +2. It's practically a flash sale.
  • Day 21+ · Harvest Window. The holder can voluntarily harvest: mint a 1/1 "Grower" souvenir (guaranteed Legendary) and release the potato at 25% of last price. A dignified exit.
  • Day 30 · Treasury Buyback. Still no sale, no harvest? Anyone can trigger the treasury to buy it back at 40% of last price. Game never freezes.
WHY THIS MATTERS

Spectators hunt for sprouts. A sprouted potato is a public bargain. When the potato enters Stage 3 or 4, it becomes the single hottest thing on the site — the crowd piles in and someone wins the snipe.

Quick FAQ

Do I need to do anything to "defend" the potato?

No. You can't. The game has no defense mechanic — anyone can snipe you at any time by paying your asking price. Your only control is the price you set. Set it high, you earn more when sniped but wait longer. Set it low, you get sniped faster.

If the potato is always for sale, what does "holding" even mean?

It means occupying the throne. You haven't been sniped yet. Every minute you're still there, your future Pot slice and souvenir rarity grow. You're not hiding the potato — you're visible, wearing a target, getting paid for the risk.

So what is the buyer actually buying?

They're buying a fresh seat and a fresh timer. They're not buying any portion of the existing Pot — that belongs to whoever gets sniped next. They are buying a position to earn their own future Pot slice and souvenir, and if the potato is sprouted, they're also buying a Sprout Boost.

Can I set the asking price super high to stay longer?

Yes — up to the cap (10× your purchase price). But the longer you sit, the more likely the potato sprouts, and then your asking price starts decaying automatically. So high asking prices give you time but aren't bulletproof.

Can the holder claim the Pot while still holding?

No. The Pot is paid out at the moment of being sniped, not before. It's your exit bonus, not a yield you can siphon. This is intentional — it means your Pot slice is always on the line.

What stops one person from farming the Pot forever?

A wallet cap: no wallet can claim more than 2× Tier 4 Pot payouts in any 90-day rolling window. After that, further Tier 4 hits downgrade to Tier 2 rates. Plus every round trip costs them protocol fees and rooters know their patterns.

Why would a spectator root rather than just buy the potato?

Rooting costs as little as 0.001 ETH. Buying the potato might cost several ETH. Rooting lets casual players bet on patience without buying the throne — and the winning rooters earn a boost they can use later when they eventually buy. It's the on-ramp.

Two Rules You'll Hear Again

NOT A RULE

"Hold out." There's no holding out. The potato is always for sale and you cannot refuse. You're on the clock from the moment you buy.

THE ACTUAL RULE

"Stay seated longer than the next player expects." That's the entire skill of the game — pricing your seat so buyers hesitate just long enough for your Pot slice to grow.
