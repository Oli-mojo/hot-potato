The Whole Game In 30 Seconds

The Hot Potato is one NFT, always for sale. One player holds it at a time. Anyone can buy it from the current holder at any moment by paying the asking price.

When you get bought out, you receive two things:

  • 95% of the ETH the buyer paid — sent to your wallet to withdraw (5% goes to the creator)
  • A Souvenir NFT — a unique piece of art whose rarity is determined by how long you held and how much the buyer overpaid
You can't refuse a sale.
You just try to stay on as long as possible.

That's the whole game. Set your asking price, wait, get bought out, collect your proceeds and your souvenir. The longer you hold, the rarer the souvenir — but hold too long and the asking price may start to feel like a barrier.

Buying the Potato

To buy, you pay the current asking price and set a new asking price for the next buyer. Two rules govern your new price:

  • Minimum increase. Your asking price must be at least a set percentage above what you paid. The percentage scales down as prices rise to keep the game accessible at higher valuations.
  • Maximum cap. Your asking price cannot exceed 10× what you paid. This prevents anyone from freezing the game with an astronomical price.

STRATEGY

Set your ask high to buy yourself more time on the throne — the next buyer needs to pay more, so you wait longer and accumulate rarer souvenir odds. Set it low and you flip quickly for a smaller but faster profit. The 10× cap ensures nobody can orphan the game permanently.

Your Souvenir — What You Leave With

Every time the potato changes hands, the outgoing holder receives a Souvenir NFT — a unique piece of AI-generated art that commemorates their hold. The art style and rarity tier are determined on-chain at the moment of sale and cannot be changed afterwards.

Two things determine your souvenir's rarity: how long you held and how much the next buyer overpaid.

⏱️
HOLD DURATION
Your base rarity tier

The clock starts the moment you buy and stops the moment someone buys from you. Longer holds unlock better base odds.

Your hold duration sets your base tier — the starting point for the rarity roll.

💸
BUYER OVERPAY
A bonus boost for you

When the next buyer pays more than your asking price, that overpayment gives you a rarity boost — up to +4 tiers.

So an eager buyer who throws in extra ETH is actually doing you a favour.

The roll uses a combination of block randomness, timestamp, buyer address, and transfer count — it is transparent and verifiable on-chain, but not cryptographically unpredictable. In practice, no player can meaningfully influence the outcome of their own roll.

Extra Rarity Boosts

On top of hold duration and buyer overpay, the site offers four ways to stack additional rarity boosts onto your next souvenir. These are applied at generation time — they don't change the on-chain rarity tier, but they shift the art quality and metadata rarity upward.

🎟️
PROMO CODES

Social codes shared via Discord or Twitter give +1 rarity boost. Apply a code before you buy — it locks in and activates when you eventually get bought out.

🏆
LOYALTY BOOST

Hold the potato multiple times and build a loyalty counter. Once you have unclaimed holds, claim your loyalty boost before your next buy — up to +3 for three or more previous holds.

🤝
REFERRAL

Share your referral code with a new buyer. When they use it, you both get +1. Each wallet can redeem one referral code.

🔥
TRADE-IN

Burn an existing souvenir NFT to receive a trade-in code. The code gives a rarity boost scaled to the burned souvenir's tier — Common gives +1, Rare +2, Epic +3, Legendary +4.

Quick FAQ

Can I refuse to sell?

No. Anyone can buy the potato from you at any time by paying your asking price. You can't block, pause, or delay a sale. Your only control is the price you set.

How do I collect my ETH after being bought out?

Your proceeds (95% of what the buyer paid) are credited to your wallet in the contract and collected via a withdraw transaction — they're not pushed automatically. This protects the game from broken payment receivers. The UI shows your pending balance and lets you withdraw with one click.

What determines my souvenir's rarity?

Two on-chain factors: how long you held (sets your base tier) and how much the buyer overpaid (adds up to +4 tiers). Off-chain boosts from promo codes, loyalty, referrals, and trade-ins apply on top at generation time.

What's the point of setting a high asking price?

Time. A higher price makes the next buyer pay more and hesitate longer, giving your hold timer more time to tick up — which shifts your rarity odds toward Epic and Legendary. The tradeoff is you earn more ETH but wait longer to collect it.

Can I set my asking price to anything?

Within two bounds. It must be at least the tiered minimum above what you paid (5–25% depending on price). And it can be at most 10× what you paid. The cap prevents someone from permanently freezing the game with an impossible price.

If the buyer overpays, who benefits?

You, the current holder. The overpay boosts your souvenir rarity — not the buyer's. The buyer's rarity boost is banked for their souvenir, which they'll receive when they eventually get bought out. Everyone wins from an eager overpayer.

Are souvenir rarity odds visible before I get bought out?

Yes. The site shows your live rarity preview — your current hold tier, any boost from when you bought, and the resulting odds — updated in real time as your hold timer ticks up. You always know what you're rolling for.

Can I trade or sell my souvenir NFTs?

Yes. Souvenirs are standard ERC-721 tokens and appear on OpenSea and other marketplaces. You can also burn them via the site's trade-in feature to get a boost code for your next souvenir instead.

How exactly is rarity randomness generated — is it truly random?

Rarity is determined on-chain at the moment of sale using a hash of four inputs: block randomness (prevrandao), the timestamp, the buyer's address, and the total number of transfers. This is transparent — anyone can verify the inputs and the result on-chain. It is not, however, cryptographically unpredictable: a block validator could theoretically try to influence block randomness to favour a particular outcome. In practice the economic incentive to do so is extremely low relative to the cost, and no normal player has any influence over it at all. We consider this an acceptable tradeoff for a game at this scale.

The Two Rules

NOT A RULE

"Hold out." You can't. The potato is always for sale and you cannot refuse. The moment you buy it, someone else can buy it from you. There's no defence mechanic.

THE ACTUAL RULE

"Set your price so that waiting longer is worth it." A higher ask buys you time on the throne — more time means rarer odds and more ETH when you're eventually bought out. Every second you're still holding, your souvenir is getting more valuable.
